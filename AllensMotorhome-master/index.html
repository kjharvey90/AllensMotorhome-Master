Trip 1: Middle-Europe

How thorough you are when it comes to planning your motorhome trip is completely up to you. Personally I took the option of buying an Aires Guide for both France and Italy. On estimate I drove a total of 6000km, starting at Portsmouth and arriving back in Hull. The beauty of Europe is it's accessibility for motorhomes. Aires stops are ok for a night and have water, emptying points, even electric hook-ups (Be careful though, The guide may say electric hook-up but there's usually only one point... first come firsst serve!). Alternatively you can spend a few days on a campsite. I would recommend purchasing an ASCI guide and camping card. You can simply select a site you wish to stay at, book over the phone or via the app and away you go!

Before you start travelling...

  1. Plan! - To be on the safe side at least have an idea of where there and sites and stops suitable for your motorhome (Get the guides!).
  2. Prepare for the EU - Research the laws and essential items needed to travel in your motorhome (Coming soon to Wheresallen.
  3. Safety first - If there's not another motorhome there... beware! Don't be the only motorhome on a site or park, there's usually a good reason
  4. Take staple ingredients- Think about being cost effective, especially with your food. I recommend packet mixes ( e.g. Maggi or Schwartz packets), these are light, easy to store and will make some lovely meals with fresh ingredients.
  5. Stay in touch - I would recommend adding a GPS tracking device to your motorhome, it certainly put my mind at ease being a lone traveller. My family were able to keep track of my whereabouts and even help find local sites
  6. Try everything once - there's no time like the present, wherever you go there's always something to do or try... take cookery classes for example! Allow for unexpected adventures in your budget.
